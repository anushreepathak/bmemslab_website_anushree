ECE 20-260-641 SILICON SEMICONDUCTOR MICROFABRICATION LAB FOR MEMS

Catalog Data:
SILICON SEMICONDUCTOR MICROFABRICATION LAB FOR MEMS
Micromachined device and structure fabrication, surface or bulk micromachining techniques, photolithography, oxidation, thin film deposition, thermal boron doping, ion implantation, isotropic and anisotropic etching, wafer bonding, and sensor and actuator characterization. Credit: 3

Textbook:
Class notes and handouts

Reference:
R. S. Muller et al (Eds), Microsensors: IEEE Press, 1991
S. M. Sze, VLSI Technology, McGraw-Hill1, 1987

Coordinator:
Chong H. Ahn, Assistant Professor of Electrical and Computer Engineering and Computer science.

Goals:
This course is designed to teach the state-of-the-art micromachining techniques and to provide hands-on experience in micromachined device fabrication and characterization for MEMS applications. Typical processes and process flow for micromachining (fabrication of micro-scale movable part such as microsensors and microactuators) will be discussed and demonstrated. A proto-type pressure sensor with micromachined micro-diaphragm will be actually fabricated and characterized to illustrate the micromachining processes.

Prerequisites by topic:
Senior and graduate status in engineering or physical science.

Laboratory projects: (including major items of equipment and instrumentation used):
  • 1. Introduction to MRL (equipment, safety, operating procedures), micromachines, microsensors, microactuators, and MEMS
  • 2. Principles of micromachining techniques
  • 3. Mask design, layout and development
  • 4. Photolithography (thin and thick film)
  • 5. Dry or wet thermal oxidation and non-thermal oxidation of silicon wafers
  • 6. Thin film deposition: metal, insulation layer, and active material deposition (thermal- evaporator, e-beam evaporator, sputter, CVD, and PECVD)
  • 7. Thermal boron doping and ion implantation
  • 8. Dry etching (RIE) and wet etching (isotropic, anisotropic etching, KOH, EDP, Hydrazine, and electrochemical etching)
  • 9. Prototype pressure sensor fabrication process sequence
  • 10. Wafer bonding
  • 11. Pressure sensor characterization
